# Task 04 – Twin Paradox

## Problem Statement

Twin A remains on Earth. Twin B travels at $v = 0.9c$ for 10 years according to Earth's clocks and then returns.

1. Calculate the proper time elapsed for twin B.
2. Determine how many years younger twin B is upon return.
3. Analyze the symmetry of the reference frames.
4. Identify the moment the symmetry is broken.
5. Interpret the result using Minkowski spacetime.

## Theory

The proper time $\tau$ for a moving observer is related to the coordinate time $t$ by:

$$
d\tau = \frac{dt}{\gamma}
$$

For a round trip with constant speed, the total elapsed proper time is:

$$
\Delta \tau = \frac{\Delta t_{total}}{\gamma}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Calculation of proper time for Twin B

First, find the Lorentz factor for $v = 0.9c$:

$$
\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - 0.9^2}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{0.19}} \approx 2.294
$$

If 10 years pass on Earth during the outbound leg and 10 years on the return (total $\Delta t = 20$ years), the time for Twin B is:

$$
\Delta \tau = \frac{20}{2.294} \approx 8.72\,\text{years}
$$

### 2. Age difference

The difference in aging is:

$$
\Delta \text{age} = 20 - 8.72 = 11.28\,\text{years}
$$

### 3. Symmetry of frames

In special relativity, motion is relative. However, the symmetry is not complete here because Twin B must accelerate to turn around and return to Earth.

### 4. Breaking of symmetry

The symmetry is broken at the turnaround point. Twin A stays in a single inertial frame for the entire duration, while Twin B must switch from one inertial frame (outbound) to another (inbound).

### 5. Minkowski interpretation

In Minkowski spacetime, the "distance" between two events is the spacetime interval. The straight worldline of Twin A represents the path of maximal proper time between the two events (departure and return). Any deviated path (like Twin B's) results in a shorter proper time.

## Final Result

- Proper time for Twin B: $\approx 8.72\,\text{years}$
- Age difference: Twin B is $11.28\,\text{years}$ younger.

## Interpretation

The "paradox" is resolved by recognizing that Twin B is not in an inertial frame throughout the entire journey. Acceleration is absolute, and the twin who undergoes acceleration experiences less proper time.
