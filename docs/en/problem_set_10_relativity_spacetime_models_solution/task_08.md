# Task 08 – Gravitational Redshift

## Problem Statement

1. Derive the shift for Earth's surface in a weak field.
2. Calculate the relative frequency change.
3. Interpret as gravitational time dilation.
4. Estimate for a neutron star.

## Theory

In a weak gravitational potential $\Phi$, the frequency shift is:

$$
\frac{\Delta f}{f} \approx \frac{\Delta \Phi}{c^2}
$$

For Earth, $\Phi = -GM/r$.

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Earth's Surface Shift

For a height $h$:

$$
\Delta \Phi = g h
$$

$$
\frac{\Delta f}{f} = \frac{gh}{c^2}
$$

### 2. Relative change

For $h = 100\,\text{m}$:

$$
\frac{\Delta f}{f} = \frac{9.8 \times 100}{(3 \times 10^8)^2} \approx 1.09 \times 10^{-14}
$$

### 3. Neutron Star Estimate

For a neutron star ($M \approx 1.4 M_{\odot}$, $R \approx 10\,\text{km}$):

$$
\frac{\Delta f}{f} \approx \frac{GM}{Rc^2} \approx 0.2
$$

## Interpretation

Gravity slows down time. A clock in a stronger gravitational field (lower potential) ticks slower than a clock in a weaker field. This is verified by GPS satellites, which must account for this shift.
