# Task 02 – Length Contraction

## Problem Statement

A rocket has a proper length $L_0 = 120\,\text{m}$ and moves at $v = 0.6c$.

1. Calculate the length observed by a stationary observer.
2. Determine the percentage of contraction.
3. Determine if the observer in the rocket sees the contraction.
4. Identify the invariant quantity.
5. Interpret the "rocket in a tunnel" paradox.

## Theory

Length contraction is the phenomenon where the length of an object measured by an observer in relative motion is shorter than its proper length. This contraction occurs only in the direction of motion.

The observed length $L$ is:

$$
L = \frac{L_0}{\gamma} = L_0 \sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}
$$

## Step-by-Step Solution

### 1. Observed length calculation

First, calculate the Lorentz factor for $v = 0.6c$:

$$
\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - (0.6)^2}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{0.64}} = \frac{1}{0.8} = 1.25
$$

Now, apply the contraction formula:

$$
L = \frac{120}{1.25} = 96\,\text{m}
$$

### 2. Percentage of contraction

The reduction in length relative to the original length is:

$$
\text{Contraction} = \left(1 - \frac{L}{L_0}\right) \times 100\%
$$

$$
\text{Contraction} = \left(1 - \frac{96}{120}\right) \times 100\% = 20\%
$$

### 3. Perception within the rocket

An observer inside the rocket is at rest relative to the rocket. Therefore, they measure the proper length $L_0$. They do not see the rocket contracted; however, they would see the external universe contracted in the direction of their motion.

### 4. Invariant quantity

The proper length $L_0$ is the length measured in the object's rest frame and is an intrinsic property. In the broader context of spacetime, the spacetime interval $s^2$ is the invariant quantity across all inertial frames.

### 5. The "Rocket in a Tunnel" paradox

If a 120m rocket moves fast enough to contract to 96m, it should fit inside a 100m tunnel. However, from the rocket's perspective, the tunnel is contracted. The paradox is resolved by the **relativity of simultaneity**. The events of the front and back of the rocket being inside the tunnel are not simultaneous in all frames.

## Final Result

- Observed length: $96\,\text{m}$
- Percentage contraction: $20\%$

## Interpretation

Length contraction is a direct consequence of time dilation and the constancy of the speed of light. To maintain $c$ as a constant, if time slows down for a moving object, the distance it covers (and its own physical extent) must be measured differently by external observers.
