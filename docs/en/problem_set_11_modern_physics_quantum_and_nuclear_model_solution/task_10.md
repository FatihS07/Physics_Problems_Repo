# Task 10 – The Pauli Exclusion Principle

## Problem Statement

Write a note on a fundamental topic in modern physics.

## Theory

The Pauli Exclusion Principle states that no two fermions (particles with half-integer spin, like electrons) can occupy the same quantum state simultaneously within the same quantum system.

$$
\psi(\mathbf{r}_1, \mathbf{r}_2) = -\psi(\mathbf{r}_2, \mathbf{r}_1)
$$

## Physical Implications

### 1. Atomic Structure
If not for this principle, all electrons in an atom would collapse into the $1s$ ground state. Instead, they fill successive shells, giving rise to the periodic table and the diversity of chemical properties.

### 2. Degeneracy Pressure
In dense stars, electrons or neutrons are squeezed so tightly that they are forced into high-energy states even at low temperatures. This "degeneracy pressure" supports **White Dwarfs** and **Neutron Stars** against gravitational collapse.

## Final Result

The principle is a consequence of the spin-statistics theorem and the requirement for the total wavefunction of fermions to be anti-symmetric under particle exchange.

## Interpretation

This principle is the reason "matter takes up space." It provides the structural stability of the universe, preventing atoms from merging into a single point of infinite density.
